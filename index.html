CHRONIC KIDNEY DISEASE

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a gradual loss of kidney function over time, often caused by diabetes or high blood pressure. It can lead to serious health issues if not managed properly.

HYPERTENSION

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a condition where the force of blood against artery walls is too high, increasing the risk of heart disease, stroke, and kidney problems.

HAEMODIALYSIS CATHETER

A hemodialysis catheter is a tube inserted into a vein to provide access for dialysis treatment, allowing the removal of waste, excess fluids, and toxins from the blood in patients with kidney failure.

ARTERIOVENOUS FISTULA

An arteriovenous fistula (AVF) is a surgical connection between an artery and a vein, commonly created for dialysis patients to provide efficient blood flow during hemodialysis treatment.

KIDNEY TRANSPLANT

A kidney transplant is a surgical procedure to replace a failing kidney with a healthy one from a donor, helping patients with end-stage kidney disease regain normal kidney function.

IJV CATHETERIZATION

IJV catheterization involves inserting a catheter into the internal jugular vein for various medical procedures, such as central venous access, medication delivery, or monitoring in critical care patients.

DIABETES

Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body either can’t produce enough insulin or can’t use it effectively, leading to high blood sugar levels. It can result in serious health complications if unmanaged.

GLOMERULAR DISEASE

Glomerular disease refers to damage to the glomeruli, the kidney’s filtering units, which can lead to protein or blood in the urine, swelling, and impaired kidney function. It may result from various conditions.
